Crustacea

Basic information:

Crustaceans form a large, diverse subphylum of phylum Arthropoda. This class includes some of the more familiar Arthropods including Barnacles, Copepods, Crabs, Prawns,Lobsters and Wood Lice. Despite such an extraordinary diversity of species, many crustaceans have a similar structure and way of life.

  • Population: 69420
  • Behaviour: The majority of them are marine-dwelling. Terrestrial species such as woodlice and pill bugs are believed to have evolved from marine species. Some species are free swimming while others are specialized at crawling or burrowing in soft sediments.
  • Habitat: ocean
  • Evolution: The body of crustacean is composed of segments which are grouped into three region: the cephalon or head, the pereon, or thorax and pleon or abdomen. The head and the thorax may be fused together to form a cephalothorax. Their body is protected by hard exoskeleton.
  • Distribution: Many crustaceans are consumed by Humans, the vast majority of this output is of decapod Crustaceans, Crabs, Lobster, Shrimp, Crawfish and Prawns. non decapod crustaceans are not widely consumed.
