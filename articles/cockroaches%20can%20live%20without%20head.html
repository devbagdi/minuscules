Cockroaches can live without head

09/07/2020      2 comments

    To most of the people out there, cockroaches are already very revulsive and creepy organisms. After knowing the fact that it can live for several days without a head, it definitely makes one’s skin crawl! Well, there are several reasons that this little creature can survive without head and we can’t.


The very first thing that may strike our mind by reading head being chopped off is: why the cockroach doesn’t bleed to death? The answer to this is that, unlike humans, cockroaches have an open circulatory system, which means they have a system for distributing blood through their bodies that doesn’t depend on a closed network of arteries and veins. Their blood doesn’t pump under pressure like ours. Hence, even when the head is cut-off, blood would not be forced out, and a clot would be formed around the neck.


The second thing is why the cockroach won’t suffocate to death? Well, again, unlike humans, cockroaches do not have a fixed organ for breathing. Rather, they breathe through the small pores, called spiracles, that don’t need a brain to direct them.


The next thing is how cockroach manages to live without a brain? Yes, cockroaches do have a brain and they use them! However, they don’t need it that much. Cockroaches have nerve tissues that control reflexes throughout their bodies, and these are distributed within each body segment. Thus, even if the roach loses its brain, the nervous tissues around the body continue doing their job. These little devilish creatures would move around as normally as they do with the head.


So the next time you see a firefly, keep in mind that its flickering isn’t just a wonder of the night. It’s also a unique complex process that can be very beneficial as well.

Thus, their bioluminescence can be considered a way of communication. Also, Unlike a light bulb, which produces a lot of heat in addition to light, a firefly's light is "cold light" without a lot of energy being lost as heat. This is necessary because if a firefly's light-producing organ got as hot as a light bulb, the firefly would not survive the experience.


On the other hand, Luciferin that is extractable from fireflies is a very important compound, it is considered to have potential to be used in detection of specific enzymes in the brains of living rats, which could someday offer doctors another window into the human brain. Firefly luciferin is already proving to be a useful tool in imaging human tumors and developing cancer-fighting drugs.

As adults, many fireflies have flash patterns unique to their species and use them to identify other members of their species as well as to discriminate between members of the opposite sex. Thus, their bioluminescence can be considered a way of communication. Also, Unlike a light bulb, which produces a lot of heat in addition to light, a firefly's light is "cold light" without a lot of energy being lost as heat. This is necessary because if a firefly's light-producing organ got as hot as a light bulb, the firefly would not survive the experience.


On the other hand, Luciferin that is extractable from fireflies is a very important compound, it is considered to have potential to be used in detection of specific enzymes in the brains of living rats, which could someday offer doctors another window into the human brain. Firefly luciferin is already proving to be a useful tool in imaging human tumors and developing cancer-fighting drugs.


So the next time you see a firefly, keep in mind that its flickering isn’t just a wonder of the night. It’s also a unique complex process that can be very beneficial as well.
