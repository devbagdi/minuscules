Insects: Our Tiny Saviour

07/07/2020      2 comments

    The world is full of different type of creatures and there size vary from few millimeters to some meters. Some organism attracts us towards them and some makes us afraid of them. But no matter what they are, they all affect our lives directly or indirectly.


Insects are also such creatures on our mother earth. They are one of the creatures on earth which have a wide variety of species and that is approximately around 6.1 million species among them approximately 1.5 million species are only of beetles. It’s quite awful and hard to imagine the benefits of these insects on our earth. But these insects aerate the soil, pollinate blossoms .Many insects, especially beetles, are scavengers, feeding on dead animals and fallen trees, thereby recycling nutrients back into the soil. As decomposers, insects help create top soil, the nutrient-rich layer of soil that helps plants grow. Even the mosquitoes doing buzzing sound in your ears and disturbing your sleep has its own benefits.


The mosquitoes which mostly feed on plant nectar are important pollinators. They are also a food source for birds and bats and are important component of our food chain and even there absence can affect us badly. In spite of all these positive attributes some insects may be harmful for us and can cause problem like bite of female mosquito (Aedes aegypti) can cause dengue fever, locust swarms devastate crops.


But unfortunately most people are more aware of the insects that cause problems than the beneficial insects. Uninformed people think that all insects are bad and all are in need of control. We must always keep in mind that the good done by the many beneficial insects far outweighs any bad caused by a few pest species.


We might think that humans mainly depends upon the plants for their survival but we might wonder from this fact that we depend upon insects too, even more than they depend upon us. So, if there are no insects on this earth than after a certain interval of time there will be no humans too.


Author:

RITISH KHETARPAL is Bachelor's Student in the Indian institute of Science Education and Research
