Crustaceans

05/07/2020

    crustaceans form a large, DIVERSE SUBPHYLUM of phylum Arthropoda. This class includes some of the more familiar Arthropods including Barnacles, Copepods, Crabs, Prawns,Lobsters and Wood Lice. Despite such an extraordinary diversity of species, many crustaceans have a similar structure and way of life.


Ecology

The majority of them are marine-dwelling. Terrestrial species such as woodlice and pill bugs are believed to have evolved from marine species. Some species are free swimming while others are specialized at crawling or burrowing in soft sediments.


Structure

The body of crustacean is composed of segments which are grouped into three region: the cephalon or head, the pereon, or thorax and pleon or abdomen. The head and the thorax may be fused together to form a cephalothorax. Their body is protected by hard exoskeleton.


Breeding

Most species are dieicious, but some are Hermaphrodite. Fertilization is usually internal through direct copulation. The fertilized eggs then mature either in a specialized brood chamber in some part of female's body.


Body Mechanism

Most crustaceans have special gills that serve as a means of obtaining oxygen . Simple excretory organs ensure the removal of the body wastes such as ammonia and urea. all crustaceans are proabaly capable of detecting a light source but in some of the other species , definite shapes and strucres may also be dedicated.


Commercial Use

Many crustaceans are consumed by Humans, the vast majority of this output is of decapod Crustaceans, Crabs, Lobster, Shrimp, Crawfish and Prawns. non decapod crustaceans are not widely consumed.
